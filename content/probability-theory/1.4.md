{{% definition name="Partition" %}}

We say $B\_1, B\_2..., B\_n$ forms a partition of the sample space $\Omega$ if they are pairwise disjoint and their union is $\Omega $. That is,

$$B\_i \cap B\_j = \emptyset, \forall i\neq j$$

$$\bigcup\_{i=1}^n B\_i = \Omega$$

{{% /definition %}}

{{% theorem name=" Total Probability Theorem" %}}

Let $A\_1, ..., A\_n $ be a partition of a sample space $\Omega $ with a probability law $P $. Then

$$\forall B \subseteq \Omega, \b{P}(B) = \sum\_{ k=1 }^{ n } \b{P}(B \cap A\_k) = \sum\_{ k=1 }^{ n } \b{P}(A\_k) \b{P}(B | A\_k)$$

{{% /theorem %}}

{{% theorem name="Bayes' Rule" %}}

Let $A\_1 , A\_2, ..., A\_n$ be a partition of the sample space $\Omega $. Then, $\forall B \subseteq \Omega , \b{P}(B) \neq 0$

$$\b{P}(A|B)= \frac{\b{P}(B|A\_j)\b{P}(A\_j)} {\sum\_{ i=1 }^{ n } \b{P}(B|A\_i)\b{P}(A\_i)}$$

{{% /theorem %}}
