Recall \beta is a basis of V if \beta is an independent subset that generates V.

Corollary

Let $\beta = \set{v_1, v_2, …, v_{n}}$ be a basis of $V$. Suppose $\set{w_1, w_2, …, w_{n}}$ is linearly independent, then $w_1, w_2, …, w_{n}$ is also a basis of $V$.

Equivalently,
If $\dim V = n$, if $\abs{S} = n, S$ is linearly independent, then $S$ is a basis.

Proof [expand]

Let $\dim V = n, \beta$ is a basis of $V , L = \set{w_1, w_2, …, w_{n}}, L$ is linearly independent.

$\exists H \subseteq \beta$ s.t. $\abs{H}= n-m=n-n =0$ and $\spa{L \cup H} = V = V$.

This implies that $H = \emptyset\ (\abs{H}=0)$

and $V = \spa{L \cup H} = \spa{L \cup \emptyset}$

$L$ is linearly independent and $L$ generates $V$\implies $L$ is a basis.
